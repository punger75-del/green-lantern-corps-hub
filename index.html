Era Chronicles

Golden Age (1940-1951) Alan Scott was a rail engineer who forged a ring from the "Starheart" meteor[cite: 11, 12]. His power was magical and limited by a weakness to wood[cite: 13, 18].
Silver Age (1959-1970) The mythos shifted to science fiction with test pilot Hal Jordan, who was selected by the dying alien Abin Sur to join the intergalactic Green Lantern Corps[cite: 23, 24].
Modern & Rebirth Eras After the destruction of Coast City, Hal Jordan became the villain Parallax[cite: 43]. The lore was later retconned to reveal Parallax as a fear entity that possessed Jordan[cite: 49, 92].

Earth's Protectors

Hal Jordan & John Stewart Hal is a fearless test pilot[cite: 82]. John Stewart is an architect and Marine veteran who has led both the Corps and the Justice League[cite: 35, 82].
Kyle Rayner & Jessica Cruz Kyle is an artist known as "The Torchbearer"[cite: 44, 83]. Jessica Cruz is the first human female Green Lantern, famous for overcoming intense anxiety[cite: 84].
Teen Lantern (Keli Quintela) An 11-year-old Bolivian girl who "hacked" into the Green Lantern power source using an advanced alien gauntlet[cite: 79, 1005, 1051].

Iconic Alien Legends

Kilowog The Bolovaxian primary drill instructor for the Corps and the last of his species[cite: 85, 86].
Tomar-Re A Xudarian scientist and close friend of Hal Jordan[cite: 790, 797]. He famously failed to save the planet Krypton from destruction[cite: 778, 796].
Mogo A sentient, living planet that guides power rings to new recruits across the universe[cite: 88, 816].
